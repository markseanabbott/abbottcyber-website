Retail Security

Multi-location retail has a consistency problem attackers know how to exploit

Retailers collect payment card data, loyalty PII, and transaction records across dozens or hundreds of locations - each with its own technology, its own vendors, and its own level of security maturity. That inconsistency is the attack surface.

Where retail gets hit

The threat landscape for retail operators

💳

POS skimming and tampering

Physical and logical attacks against point-of-sale systems remain among the most common retail incidents. Both in-store card readers and e-commerce payment flows are targets - often simultaneously in omnichannel environments.

🏆

Loyalty program credential theft

Loyalty accounts with stored value or reward points are targeted through credential stuffing - using breached username/password combinations from other platforms to access your loyalty system. Large programs with high-value balances are primary targets.

🔌

Third-party integration risk

E-commerce platforms, logistics providers, payment processors, and inventory management systems all have access to your transaction data. Each integration is a potential entry point - and most don't have security obligations written into the vendor contract.

🗺

Inconsistent controls across locations

When each location has been set up differently - different POS configurations, different network arrangements, different staff practices - attackers identify the weakest property and use it as the entry point for broader lateral movement.

🛒

E-commerce platform exposure

Online stores on Shopify, WooCommerce, or custom platforms introduce web application risk - skimming scripts, insecure plugins, administrative credential theft, and transaction data exposure in transit or at rest.

📧

Business email compromise

Retail finance and operations teams are targeted for fraudulent wire transfers and vendor payment redirection. Multi-location structures often mean less oversight of individual transaction approvals, increasing exposure.

Compliance context

The regulatory and contractual obligations retailers carry

Retail compliance obligations are layered - PCI DSS from payment brands, state and provincial privacy laws from where you operate, and sector-specific breach notification requirements that activate at different thresholds depending on jurisdiction.

Most multi-location retailers have PCI obligations they've partially addressed but not fully validated. Scope assumptions that made sense when the business was smaller often don't hold as e-commerce, loyalty programs, and third-party integrations expand the cardholder data environment in ways that weren't reviewed.

💳
PCI DSS Payment card data is the primary compliance driver. Scope validation, network segmentation review, and SAQ accuracy are the three areas where most retailers have unresolved gaps.
🌎
CCPA & state privacy laws California, Colorado, Virginia, and other states have consumer data privacy requirements with breach notification timelines and consumer rights obligations. If you sell to US residents, these apply to you regardless of where you're incorporated.
🍁
PIPEDA & Bill C-27 Canadian privacy legislation for customer data collected across your locations. Breach notification to the Privacy Commissioner and affected individuals is required at a specific risk threshold.
🔒
Cyber insurance Underwriters are tightening retail-specific requirements around POS security, multi-factor authentication, and network segmentation. Pre-renewal readiness review prevents gaps from affecting coverage.
