Security Awareness Training

Phishing is still the #1 way attackers get in.
Training that runs once a year doesn't fix that.

Most security awareness programs are built to satisfy a compliance checkbox, not to change how people behave. Generic annual modules and a signed policy don't move the needle. What changes behavior is training that's specific to how your people actually work - paired with simulations that show you what changed.

Talk about your training needs
The problem

Compliance training and security training are not the same thing

A completed training module tells you that an employee clicked through a presentation. It doesn't tell you whether they'd recognize a credential phishing attempt at 4pm on a busy Friday, hand over payment details to a convincing BEC email, or paste a client file into a free AI tool because no one told them not to.

The behaviors that cause incidents aren't covered by generic compliance training - because that training isn't designed to address your specific environment, your real threat actors, or the way your staff actually uses technology.

Security awareness that works is relevant, repeated, and measured. It focuses on what your people are most likely to encounter, not what the vendor template covers.

🎣
Phishing is the primary initial access vector The majority of breaches start with a phishing email or credential theft. This is not a sophisticated attack - it's a people problem with a people solution.
📊
Completion rates measure nothing Knowing that 95% of staff completed the module tells you nothing about whether behavior changed. Phishing simulations do.
🤖
AI tools are the new shadow risk Staff using unapproved AI tools with client data, financial records, or confidential communications is a behavior problem - one that most awareness programs haven't caught up to yet.
🔄

What we deliver

Training built for your environment, not a generic audience

🎓

Role-based sessions

Different staff face different threats. Finance teams need to recognize BEC and payment fraud. Front desk staff need to handle social engineering at the desk. Remote workers need to understand credential hygiene and unmanaged device risk. Training is scoped to the actual roles in your organization.

🎣

Phishing simulations

Simulated phishing campaigns that measure baseline susceptibility and track improvement over time. Results are used to refine training - not to shame individuals. The goal is a measurable reduction in click rate and a culture where staff report suspicious email rather than ignore it.

🤖

AI tool risk awareness

What's safe to put into AI tools, what isn't, and why. Staff training on your organization's AI acceptable use policy - including practical examples specific to your industry. Paired with the AI governance program for organizations building a full AI governance posture.

🧩

Social engineering and BEC

Business email compromise, invoice fraud, impersonation calls, vendor account changes. The scenarios that cost organizations real money - taught through realistic examples, not theoretical threat descriptions.

🔑

Password hygiene and MFA

Credential reuse, password managers, MFA fatigue attacks, and why "Password1!" isn't a strategy. Practical guidance that accounts for how people actually manage passwords - without demanding behavior that doesn't stick.

📈

Measurement and reporting

Pre- and post-training reporting that demonstrates actual behavior change - not just completion. Useful for cyber insurance applications, compliance documentation, and making the case to leadership that the investment worked.
