The operating environment

What makes manufacturing and construction different

Most cybersecurity frameworks were built for IT environments - office computers, servers, cloud workloads. Manufacturing and construction operations have those too, but they also have OT environments: programmable logic controllers, SCADA systems, industrial sensors, and process automation that was never designed to be internet-connected.

The problem is that it now effectively is. Enterprise networks connect to production floors for monitoring and efficiency. Remote access for vendor support opens pathways into OT systems. Business applications share infrastructure with industrial control systems. Every connection is a potential pivot point for an attacker who gets past the perimeter.

CMMC readiness

DoD supply chain compliance is no longer optional

The Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC) is the Department of Defense's framework for ensuring that contractors and suppliers in the defense industrial base meet minimum cybersecurity requirements. If your organization handles Federal Contract Information (FCI) or Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI), CMMC compliance is a contract requirement - not a recommendation.

CMMC Level 1 (17 practices) is required for organizations handling FCI. CMMC Level 2 (110 practices, aligned with NIST SP 800-171) is required for organizations handling CUI. Level 2 requires a third-party assessment by a C3PAO for most contracts - you need to be demonstrably compliant before the assessment, not during it.

1️⃣
CMMC Level 1 17 basic cybersecurity practices covering access control, identification and authentication, media protection, and incident response. Annual self-assessment with senior official affirmation.
2️⃣
CMMC Level 2 110 practices aligned with NIST SP 800-171. Required for organizations handling CUI. Third-party assessment (C3PAO) required for most DoD prime and subcontracts. Triennial re-assessment.
📋
System Security Plan (SSP) A documented SSP describing how your organization implements each NIST SP 800-171 requirement is the foundational document for Level 2 compliance. It needs to be accurate, current, and auditable.
📅
Plan of Action & Milestones (POA&M) Documenting unimplemented practices and the timeline to close them. A structured POA&M demonstrates program maturity even before all practices are implemented.

IR planning that accounts for OT-specific constraints - legacy systems that can't be taken offline, production continuity requirements, and the operational decisions that have to be made alongside the technical ones.
