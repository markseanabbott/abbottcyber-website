The exposure

The vendors in your environment

Most organizations have significantly more vendor access than they realize. PMS vendors with remote support access. OTA platforms with live API connectivity. Payment processors with cardholder data pipelines. Managed IT providers with admin credentials across the infrastructure.

Each of these is a legitimate business relationship. The problem is that most of them don't come with contractual security obligations - and many were never reviewed from a security perspective before the access was granted.

A vendor risk program doesn't mean eliminating vendors. It means knowing what you have, understanding the exposure, and making sure there's accountability on both sides.

🔌
Hospitality: PMS, OTA, and payment Your PMS provider, channel manager, OTA connections, and payment processor all have privileged access to your environment. PCI DSS requires vendor oversight. Most hotel operations don't have a formal program.
🖥
MSP: you are the vendor Your clients trust you with access to their environments. Demonstrating that you have a vendor risk program - and that you're managing third-party tooling in your own stack - is increasingly what enterprise clients and insurers require.
📄
Contracts without security clauses Most vendor contracts don't include security requirements, breach notification obligations, or right-to-audit clauses. That's a contractual gap that becomes a legal problem after an incident.
The program

What vendor risk management covers

📋

Vendor inventory

A complete map of third-party vendors with access to your environment - categorized by access level, data touched, and criticality to operations. Most organizations undercount this by 30–50%.

📊

Risk questionnaires

Security questionnaires sent to vendors based on their risk tier - covering security controls, certifications, incident response practices, and breach notification commitments. Results drive remediation and contract updates.

📝

Contract gap identification

Review of existing vendor contracts against a baseline security requirements checklist - identifying missing notification obligations, security clauses, and right-to-audit provisions.

👁

Ongoing monitoring oversight

Periodic review of vendor posture as part of a vCISO retainer - including re-assessment triggers, contract renewal reviews, and new vendor onboarding processes.
