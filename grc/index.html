What this actually means

GRC in plain terms

Governance, risk, and compliance sounds like a consulting category. In practice, it means three things: having a documented security program your organization actually follows (governance), knowing what your real risks are and how to prioritize them (risk management), and meeting the external obligations that your clients, regulators, and insurers require (compliance).

Most organizations have pieces of this in place. A few policies, some vendor-provided reports, a compliance checkbox here and there. What they usually lack is the connective tissue - the program structure that makes it defensible when someone actually looks.

That's what this work builds.

Frameworks

What each framework actually does for you

🛡

NIST CSF

The most practical risk management framework for most organizations. Covers Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover - giving you a complete program map, not just a controls checklist.

🎯

CIS Controls

18 prioritized controls that cover the highest-value risk reduction actions. The sequencing is the value - start with what matters most, not what the framework lists first.

💳

PCI DSS

Required if you process, store, or transmit cardholder data. Relevant for every hospitality operation, franchise, and multi-unit business that takes payments. Scoping is where most organizations get it wrong.

🌐

ISO 27001

The international standard for information security management. Often required by enterprise clients or required for government contracts. Pre-assessment and readiness work before formal certification engagement.

🍁

PIPEDA & Bill C-27

Canada's federal privacy legislation - both the current PIPEDA standard and the forthcoming CPPA under Bill C-27. If you collect Canadian personal information, this applies to you.

🔒

Cyber Insurance Readiness

Insurers are tightening requirements and asking harder questions at renewal. A pre-application compliance review identifies gaps before they affect your coverage or your premium.

🏥

HIPAA

The Security Rule requires a documented risk analysis, implemented policies, workforce training, and business associate oversight. Most healthcare organizations have documentation gaps that an OCR investigation would surface quickly.

🏅

CMMC Level 1 & 2

Required for organizations in the DoD supply chain. Level 1 covers basic FCI protection (17 practices). Level 2 aligns with NIST SP 800-171 (110 practices) and requires third-party assessment for most contracts.

✅

SOC 2

Often required by enterprise clients or SaaS customers wanting assurance over your security controls. Pre-assessment readiness work, control gap analysis, and evidence preparation before engaging a formal auditor.

🌎

CCPA & State Privacy Laws

California, Colorado, Virginia, and other US states have consumer data privacy requirements with breach notification timelines and consumer rights obligations. If you serve US residents, these apply regardless of where you're incorporated.

Cyber insurance readiness

Your renewal is coming. Underwriters are asking harder questions.

Cyber insurance underwriters have significantly tightened their requirements over the past three years. MFA, EDR, backup testing, incident response plans - controls that were optional are now required for coverage. If you can't demonstrate them at renewal, you're looking at higher premiums, reduced coverage, or declined applications.

A pre-renewal readiness review maps your current controls against typical underwriter questionnaires, identifies gaps, and gives you time to close them before the application process starts.
