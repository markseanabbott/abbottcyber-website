Ransomware Readiness

Hotels and multi-site businesses are disproportionately targeted. Here's why - and what to do about it.

Ransomware actors specifically target organizations where downtime is immediately visible and financially painful. A hotel with an encrypted PMS can't check guests in. An MSP under attack affects every client simultaneously. Readiness changes the outcome.

Why hospitality is targeted

Downtime is the leverage - and hotels feel it fast

Ransomware groups are rational actors. They target organizations where the cost of downtime exceeds the cost of the ransom - quickly. A hotel can absorb an IT disruption for minutes, maybe hours. When the PMS goes down at full occupancy, check-in becomes manual, housekeeping loses room status, revenue management loses rate visibility, and brand reputations start taking damage before the incident response team has even been assembled.

Multi-property groups multiply this exposure. A single successful attack can affect dozens of properties simultaneously. Recovery time objectives that look acceptable on paper rarely account for what it actually takes to restore a PMS across a portfolio.

⏰
Immediate operational impact PMS, POS, key card systems - encrypted within hours of infection. Manual operations are possible but painful. Every hour costs money and guest experience.
💰
Ransom calculation pressure Attackers calculate ransom against your downtime cost. The ransom often looks rational against the alternative - which is exactly the pressure they're creating.
📢
Brand and regulatory exposure Guest PII disclosure, card data breach notification, franchisor reporting requirements - the regulatory clock starts at detection, not at resolution.
Readiness assessment

What a ransomware readiness review covers

🔍

Program assessment

Review of current security controls, endpoint protection, network segmentation, and patching practices against the attack paths ransomware groups commonly exploit. Not theoretical - based on how actual attacks progress.

💾

Backup strategy review

Are your backups isolated from your production environment? When were they last tested? What's the actual recovery time for your PMS or core systems? Most backup strategies haven't been tested against a ransomware scenario specifically.

📋

IR plan alignment

Review of your incident response plan specifically for a ransomware scenario - decision trees, stakeholder notifications, ransom decision framework, cyber insurer activation, regulatory notification timelines.

⚡

Recovery time objectives

Realistic RTO and RPO analysis for your critical systems. Understanding how long recovery actually takes - for your specific environment, not a generic estimate - changes how you prioritize prevention and resilience investment.

🎯

Prioritized remediation

A sequenced list of improvements - ordered by the risk reduction they deliver - with realistic implementation timelines and estimated effort. Not a 50-item checklist. What actually moves the needle.

🔗

Tabletop exercise option

Test the plan under a simulated ransomware scenario before something real happens. Tabletop exercises designed specifically for the hospitality and MSP environments most likely to face this threat.
