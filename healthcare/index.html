The regulatory reality

What HIPAA actually requires

HIPAA's Security Rule requires covered entities and business associates to conduct a thorough assessment of the potential risks to the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of all electronic PHI they create, receive, maintain, or transmit. That assessment has to be documented, current, and implemented.

Most healthcare organizations have policies in a binder and an EHR that a vendor said was HIPAA-compliant. Neither satisfies the Security Rule on its own. The gap between "our EHR is compliant" and "we have a compliant HIPAA security program" is where OCR investigations find their cases.

Policies and procedures Access control, workforce sanctions, incident response, workstation security, audit controls - the policy framework that demonstrates a functioning program to an investigator.

Breach notification readiness HIPAA breach notification has specific timelines - 60 days to affected individuals and HHS for breaches affecting 500+ individuals. Being unprepared when a breach occurs makes a bad situation worse.
Ransomware in healthcare

Healthcare is among the highest-value ransomware targets. The downtime cost is not theoretical.

Ransomware attacks on healthcare providers are especially damaging because the operational impact is immediate and the pressure to restore access is acute. Patient care depends on EHR availability. Appointment scheduling, prescription management, billing - all of it stops when systems go down.

Ransomware actors know this. Healthcare organizations face higher ransom demands and shorter ultimatums precisely because the downtime cost is so visible and so immediate.

Ransomware readiness in healthcare is about reducing dwell time before detection, having a tested recovery process, and ensuring that when an incident occurs, the response is organized and fast - not improvised.

Ransomware readiness
  • Backup architecture review and recovery testing
  • Endpoint detection and response oversight
  • Network segmentation to contain blast radius
  • Identity and access management review
  • Incident response plan development and tabletop testing
  • HHS and state breach notification planning
  • Cyber insurance alignment and readiness
Technology landscape

EHR, telehealth, and medical device security

💻

EHR platform security

EHR vendors claim HIPAA compliance for their platform - but the obligation for your organization's configuration, access controls, audit logging, and workforce practices is yours. EHR security reviews identify where the vendor's compliance ends and yours begins.

📱

Telehealth security

Video platforms, patient portal integrations, and remote monitoring tools have introduced new attack surfaces that weren't part of most healthcare organizations' original security programs. Review and policy alignment for telehealth infrastructure.

🔬

Medical device and IoT advisory

Connected medical devices - infusion pumps, imaging equipment, patient monitoring systems - often run legacy operating systems and can't be patched. Network segmentation and access controls are the primary risk mitigation strategies.

OCR investigations

Being audit-ready before you need to be

The Office for Civil Rights investigates complaints and conducts random audits. When an investigation is opened, OCR asks for documentation: your risk analysis, your policies, your workforce training records, your business associate agreements. Organizations that can produce that documentation quickly are in a significantly better position than those that have to reconstruct it under investigation.

Abbott Cyber works with healthcare organizations to build and maintain the documentation that makes an OCR inquiry manageable rather than catastrophic - not as a one-time project, but as an ongoing part of the security program.
