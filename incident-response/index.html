Incident response leadership

What the first hours actually look like

An active security incident is not the time to introduce yourself. Abbott operates as incident commander in the early phase - stabilize, contain, and begin eradication while preserving evidence and managing the stakeholder communication that runs parallel to the technical response.

At the inflection point where the breach cost exceeds the deductible and the cyber insurer's team is engaged, Abbott steps into the internal consultant role - still in the room, still providing guidance, but letting the insurer's IR team take the technical lead. That transition, handled well, reduces cost and friction significantly.

CEOs always make the call on the hard decisions. Abbott's role is to give them the analysis they need to make those decisions: downtime cost projections, ransom calculation context, PII disclosure obligations, regulatory notification timelines.

After the incident

Regulatory support and remediation planning

🏛

Regulatory audit support

Guidance through regulator inquiries following a breach - Privacy Commissioner, payment brands, franchisor notifications. Understanding what you're required to disclose, what you're not, and how to navigate the process without making it worse.

🗺

Remediation planning

Post-incident remediation isn't just patching the entry point. It's a structured plan - with owners, timelines, and sequencing - that addresses root causes, closes secondary gaps exposed during the incident, and produces documentation that satisfies insurer and regulatory requirements.

📋

Post-incident review

A structured lessons-learned process after the incident is resolved - documenting what happened, how the response performed, and what needs to change in the IR plan before the next event. Not blame assignment. Process improvement.
