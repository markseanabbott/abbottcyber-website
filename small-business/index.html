The reality

The risks are real. The solutions don't have to be complicated.

Small businesses are targeted precisely because attackers know security ownership is unclear. There's no dedicated IT security person, no one reviewing vendor access, and the cyber insurance renewal gets filled out by whoever has time.

That's not negligence - it's the reality of running a business where security isn't the primary job. The question isn't whether you need security. It's what the right amount of security looks like for an organization your size.

🔒
Cyber insurance you can't answer for Insurers are asking harder questions at renewal - MFA, EDR, incident response plans, backup testing. If you're not sure how to answer, you're either over-claiming or under-representing your actual posture.
📋
Vendor and client questionnaires If a client or partner has sent you a security questionnaire, they're asking whether it's safe to work with you. Having someone who can answer those accurately - and close the gaps they reveal - is increasingly table stakes.
🚨
No one to call when something goes wrong An employee clicked a link. A system is behaving strangely. You're not sure if it's serious. Having a senior security voice available for a few hours a month means you're not figuring that out alone.
🧾
Policies that don't exist yet Acceptable use, password requirements, what to do with a lost laptop - the foundational policies most small businesses never wrote because no one told them they needed them.
What this looks like

Practical security for a business your size.

Engagements start as small as two or three hours a month. The goal is to give you the highest risk reduction for the time and budget you can actually commit - not to build an enterprise security program you don't need.

🔒

Cyber insurance support

Questionnaire support and gap remediation - so you can answer honestly and close the controls your insurer is asking about before renewal, not after a claim.

📋

Vendor & client questionnaires

Accurate, defensible responses to security questionnaires from clients or partners - with a clear view of what gaps to close so the answers stay true.

🚨

Incident triage & response

A senior security voice available when something goes wrong - to assess severity, guide immediate steps, and prevent a recoverable situation from becoming a crisis.

🧾

Foundational policy development

Acceptable use, password requirements, and incident response basics - written for how your business actually works, not adapted from an enterprise template.

🧑‍🏫

Security awareness for staff

Practical guidance on phishing, credential hygiene, AI tool risk, and what to do when something looks wrong - without the overhead of a formal training platform.

🔍

Risk assessment

A one-time review of your environment - what's exposed, what matters most, and a prioritized list of next steps sized to what you can actually do something about.

☁

M365 security posture review

If you're in Microsoft 365, there's almost certainly low-hanging fruit in your security configuration. A structured review surfaces what to fix and in what order.
