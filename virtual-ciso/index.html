The gap

You have an IT team. You don't have a security program.

There's a difference between having security tools and having a security program. Tools get purchased, configured, and left running. Without someone accountable for the program - setting standards, reviewing risks, ensuring oversight - you have the cost of security without most of the protection.

A vCISO fills that accountability gap. Not as a managed service. Not as a vendor. As a senior advisor who understands your environment, your risk tolerance, and your budget - and helps you build a program that holds up.

🎯
Strategic direction A security roadmap aligned to your actual risk - not a vendor's product roadmap or a compliance checklist.
👁
Executive-level oversight Board and leadership reporting. Translating technical risk into business terms. Being the person who tells the CEO what they actually need to hear.
📋
Policy and governance Security policies, vendor oversight, incident response planning - the structural layer that most organizations either skip or let gather dust.
🔒
Cyber insurance alignment Cyber insurers are getting specific about what they require. A vCISO helps you satisfy underwriter requirements and avoid coverage gaps.
Engagement models

Two ways to engage

📅

Monthly Hour Allotment

A set number of hours per month - typically 5 to 10 - providing ongoing strategic oversight, regular check-ins, policy review, and incident response availability. Best fit for organizations that need consistent security leadership without a predictable workload spike.

Most common engagement
📦

Project-Based

Defined scope, defined deliverable. Security program build-out, framework assessment, compliance readiness, M&A due diligence, post-incident remediation planning. Fixed timeline, clear output.

Good for specific initiatives

Who this is for

The right fit

A vCISO engagement works best for organizations that have a real security need - compliance pressure, cyber insurance requirements, a recent incident, or a growth stage that's outpaced their current security structure. It doesn't work for organizations with no budget, no appetite for change, or a team that isn't ready to act on what they hear.
