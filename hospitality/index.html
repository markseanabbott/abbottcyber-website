The threat landscape

What the attack surface actually looks like

🏨
PMS and POS systems Your property management system and point-of-sale terminals are primary targets. Many run legacy software, vendor-managed remote access, and integrations that create pathways most IT teams don't have visibility into.
🔗
OTA and channel manager connectivity Booking.com, Expedia, channel managers, CRS systems - all connected, all with varying security standards, and none under your direct control. Each integration is a potential attack vector.
👤
Loyalty PII at scale Loyalty programs aggregate guest data across properties, stays, and transactions. For attackers, this is a concentrated target. For you, it's a PIPEDA obligation - and increasingly a Bill C-27 one.
📡
Guest Wi-Fi and network segmentation Guest-facing networks that share infrastructure with operational systems are a consistent vulnerability. Proper segmentation is often missing or hasn't been tested since implementation.
🔐
Door and access control systems Physical access systems - door locks, elevator controls, key card systems - are increasingly network-connected and frequently excluded from security reviews. They belong in scope.
🎯
Ransomware targeting hotel chains Multi-property groups are attractive ransomware targets because disruption is immediate and visible - PMS outages mean manual check-in, lost bookings, and brand damage within hours of attack.
Regulatory obligations

What you're required to do - and what's coming

💳
PCI DSS If you process, store, or transmit payment card data - which every hotel does - you have PCI obligations. Scope creep is the most common problem: organizations that believe they're out of scope when they're not.
🍁
PIPEDA & Bill C-27 Canada's federal privacy law governs how guest PII is collected, stored, and used. Bill C-27 (CPPA) introduces stricter requirements and significantly higher penalty exposure. If you haven't reviewed your privacy practices since 2022, it's time.
🏢
Franchisor security standards Major hotel brands impose security requirements on franchisees - often with varying clarity and enforcement timelines. Understanding where you stand against brand standards before an audit is significantly less painful than learning about gaps during one.
🔒
Cyber insurance requirements Hospitality operations are increasingly flagged as higher-risk by underwriters. Demonstrating security controls at renewal - and knowing which controls insurers weight most heavily - directly affects your premiums and coverage.
