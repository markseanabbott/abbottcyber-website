Dark web monitoring for hospitality

Are your guests' credentials already for sale?

Loyalty program data is a high-value target. Credentials from major hotel loyalty programs regularly appear on dark web markets — often long before the organization is aware of any breach. Monitoring for exposed guest data and staff credentials provides early warning that changes your response options significantly.

For hotels collecting guest PII under PIPEDA, early detection of a data exposure also changes the legal timeline — you have an obligation to notify affected individuals and the Privacy Commissioner of Canada in certain circumstances. Knowing before the regulator or media does matters.

MSP monitoring perspective

Are your clients' credentials exposed in your stack?

Multi-tenant MSP environments are increasingly targeted for credential harvesting. Staff credentials from client environments, PSA logins, RMM access — these are all monitored in dark web sources. Knowing when client credentials appear in breach data lets you act before attackers do.

MSPs who offer dark web monitoring as a client service — surfacing exposure for client staff and executives — add a concrete security deliverable that's easy to explain and hard to argue with.
