What the risk looks like

Cyber risk during an acquisition

Most M&A processes include financial, legal, and operational due diligence. Cybersecurity due diligence is either absent, surface-level, or delegated to a general IT reviewer who doesn't know what to look for in a hotel or multi-site operation.

The gaps that get missed most often: undisclosed prior incidents, PCI DSS compliance status that doesn't reflect reality, vendor access that's been in place for years without contracts, legacy infrastructure that will cost significantly more to secure than to replace, and privacy obligations the target hasn't been meeting.

Every one of these becomes the acquirer's problem after closing.

🔍
Unknown prior incidents Breaches that weren't disclosed, ransomware that was paid and covered up, credentials that were compromised and never rotated. These aren't hypothetical - they're common findings.
💳
PCI compliance gaps Self-assessed compliance that hasn't been validated. Scope assumptions that are wrong. Cardholder data environments that are significantly larger than reported.
🔗
Vendor access without contracts PMS vendors, IT providers, OTA integrations - all with active network access, none with current security agreements. Every one of these is inherited liability.
💸
Integration cost surprises Legacy infrastructure, incompatible security tooling, and compliance gaps that require significant remediation post-close - costs that don't appear in the financial model until after the deal is signed.
The assessment

What a cybersecurity due diligence engagement covers

🏗

IT security program review

Assessment of the target's security program maturity - policies, governance, incident history, compliance status, and staff security practices. Identifies whether there's an actual program or just documentation.

🔧

Infrastructure review

Network architecture, endpoint security, identity and access management, patch management, and backup/recovery state. Identifying technical debt and integration risk before it affects the acquisition model.

🔌

Vendor risk assessment

Inventory and review of third-party vendors with access to the target's environment - contracts, access levels, security posture, and integration architecture. Identifies inherited third-party exposure.

📋

Compliance gap analysis

PCI DSS, PIPEDA, Bill C-27, cyber insurance status, franchisor security requirements - validation of compliance claims and identification of gaps that affect deal value or post-close remediation cost.

🗺

Post-merger integration planning

A structured integration roadmap - security program alignment, tool consolidation, vendor contract rationalization, and compliance harmonization across the combined entity. Sized to your timeline and integration model.

📊

Risk-adjusted findings report

A written assessment report - executive summary, detailed findings, and a risk-adjusted remediation cost estimate - suitable for deal committee review and post-close planning.
