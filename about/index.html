The background

The story is the differentiator

Mark Abbott started as a sysadmin writing web-based automation software. He reduced his department from five people to one - himself - not by cutting corners, but by building systems that didn't need babysitting. That instinct - structure the work so it runs without you - is the foundation of everything that followed.

In 2008, during the economic downturn, he stepped into an IT Director role and began overseeing IT operations for a hospitality company with one property. By the time he left, that company had grown to 120 hotels across the USA - and the scalable IT operations and security program he built is still in use today by one of the largest hospitality companies in the world.

In 2018, the company was acquired by Aimbridge Hospitality. He was pulled into a three-hour technical interview with the incoming CIO and CTO to defend the security program. He passed, and was brought on as acting CISO. Two years later, through the Interstate Hotels merger, he stepped into VP of IT Security - partnering with a global GRC CISO (HITRUST co-founder) as the combined entity pursued going public.

In 2023, he crossed to the vendor side - building a SOC and NOC, overseeing all operations for a $5M company, and running its advisory services arm, including the MSP practice. He oversaw the SOC, NOC, and MSP through divestiture. When the company contracted in 2025, he launched Abbott Cyber Consulting - a deliberate focus on quality engagements over volume.

How we work

Risk-prioritized. Always.

Every engagement starts with the same question: what actually puts this organization at risk? Not what compliance framework says you need to do first. Not what vendor is loudest this quarter. What's the realistic threat, and what's the most effective thing to do about it?

🔍

Understand first

Every engagement starts with a real picture of the environment - the tools in place, the gaps, the threat vectors specific to your industry and your setup. No assumptions, no templated assessments.

⚖️

Prioritize by risk

Security recommendations get sequenced by actual risk reduction - not compliance order, not vendor priority. The goal is the maximum defensibility for the budget and timeline you have.

🗣

Communicate clearly

Technical findings get translated for the people who need to act on them - from engineers to the C-suite. No jargon. No fear-mongering. Just a clear picture and a practical path forward.

How we think

A few things worth saying out loud

🏗
Foundation before compliance Too many organizations jump straight to NIST or CMMC without basic policies and solid infrastructure fundamentals. Frameworks built on weak foundations don't reduce risk - they just produce documents.
🧑‍💻
Human security is undervalued Email security, security awareness training, and identity environment monitoring consistently deliver more risk reduction than additional tooling. Most clients ignore this until an incident forces the conversation.
📉
You don't need to spend a tonne Vendor fear-mongering is part of the industry. You can reduce risk materially without a six-figure security stack - if you prioritize the right things. That's what this engagement is actually for.
🛡
Security should be affordable SMBs deserve real security guidance at commodity pricing. Risk reduction shouldn't require enterprise-level budgets. The supply chain problem in cybersecurity is partly a cost-access problem.
