M365 Secure Score

Most organizations are leaving significant M365 security settings disabled by default

Microsoft 365 ships with a Secure Score — a measurable indicator of your configuration against Microsoft's security recommendations. Most organizations that use M365 have never reviewed it. The findings typically include MFA gaps, legacy authentication still enabled, external sharing policies wider than intended, and alerts that aren't routing to anyone.

Penetration testing

Managing the engagement, not just scoping it

Penetration testing is often misused — ordered because a compliance framework requires it, scoped too narrowly to be meaningful, and delivered as a report that gets filed without action.

Abbott's role in penetration testing engagements is scoping and advisory oversight — ensuring the test is designed to find what matters, working with vetted pen test vendors to manage execution, and translating findings into a prioritized remediation plan that stakeholders can actually act on.

The value isn't the test. It's what happens after the report comes back.

🎯
Scope definition Defining what gets tested, what's out of bounds, and what methodology is appropriate for your environment and your compliance requirements.
🤝
Vendor management Working with vetted penetration testing firms on your behalf — managing the engagement from scoping through report delivery without you needing to evaluate pen test vendors yourself.
📋
Findings translation Translating pen test findings into a remediation roadmap — prioritized by risk, written for both technical and executive audiences, and tied to a realistic action plan.
Human-layer testing

Phishing simulations and security awareness

Email is consistently the most common attack vector. Phishing simulations measure whether your staff can identify and report suspicious email — not in training, but under realistic conditions. The results drive targeted awareness training for the staff who need it most, not blanket training for an organization that already knows what to look for.

Phishing simulations are most effective when they're tied to a security awareness program — not run as a one-time test that produces a click rate and nothing else.

  • Targeted training for staff who need it most
  • Security awareness program development
  • Integration with identity and email security controls
  • Ongoing simulation schedule to maintain awareness over time
